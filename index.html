What makes a good dataset?
  • Volume
  • Historical
  • Consistent
  • Multivariate
  • Atomic
  • Clean
  • Clear
  • Segmented
  • Known pedigree

From the book: Now You See It by Stephen Few

High Volume

Maybe you don’t need it all, but the more data the higher the chance you have something useful

To answer your question or to find something useful

Historical

The more we know of the past, the better we can understand the future

Make sense of the present by seeing the patterns from which present conditions evolved or emerged

Consistent

Keep the data consistent when external factors change

Keep track of the context to understand the meaning of the data

Multivariate

The more variables (something that can change) we have at hand, the richer the opportunity for discovery

Atomic

Specify to the lowest level of detail at which something might ever be examined

Drill down into aggregated / summarized data when useful

Clean data

Avoid “garbage in, garbage out”

Information should be accurate, free of error and complete

Clear data

Keep terms understandable

Make it easy for yourself and others what the data means

Richly segmented

Provide and consider several groupings of the data

Creating several groupings of the data beforehand provides opportunities for later

Of Known Pedigree

It is useful – sometimes critical – to know the background of our information

Establish the reliability of your source
